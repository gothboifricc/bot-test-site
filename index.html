NEET-PG

National Eligibility cum Entrance Test (Postgraduate)

The National Eligibility cum Entrance Test (Postgraduate), abbreviated as NEET (PG) is an entrance examination in India conducted by the National Board of Examinations in Medical Sciences (NBEMS) for determining eligibility of candidates for admission to postgraduate medical programmes in government or private medical colleges.

Available Programs: Doctor of Medicine (MD), Master of Surgery (MS), PG diploma, Diplomate of National Board (DNB), Doctorate of National Board (DrNB), and NBEMS diploma.

This exam replaced All India Post Graduate Medical Entrance Examination (AIPGMEE). The counselling and seat allotment is conducted by Directorate General of Health Services (DGHS).

Eligibility Criteria

For Indian Candidates (Studying in India)

Candidates must have:

  • Bachelor of Medicine, Bachelor of Surgery (MBBS) degree or provisional MBBS pass certificate as per Indian Medical Council Act
  • MBBS registration certificate issued by the Indian or state medical council
  • Completed one year of internship or will be completing the internship

For Indian Citizens (Foreign Graduates)

Indian citizens who graduated from foreign medical colleges must:

  • Qualify the Medical Council of India Screening Test (Foreign Medical Graduate Examination)
  • Be registered with Indian or state medical council
  • Have completed one year of internship or will be completing the internship

For Foreign Nationals

Foreign nationals need registration from the country where they obtained their basic medical qualification. Medical Council of India (MCI) registration is not compulsory. Temporary registration might be given on payment of application fees.

Organizing Body

The exam is conducted by National Board of Examinations, an autonomous organisation of Ministry of Health and Family Welfare, Government of India. It also conducts exams for National Eligibility cum Entrance Test for Super Specialty courses (SS) and Master of Dental Surgery (MDS).

Key Exam Features:

  • Format: Computer-based examination
  • Language: English only
  • Questions: Single response MCQs
  • Sections: 5 sections of 40 questions each
  • Time per section: 42 minutes (auto-submitted after time expires)
  • Total duration: 210 minutes (3.5 hours)

Marking Scheme:

+4 marks for each correct response

-1 mark for each wrong response (25% negative marking)

0 marks for unattempted questions

Important Note: Recent changes for NEET-PG 2024 include the sectional timing pattern where each section cannot be reviewed once the time slot is over.
